Dental MD vs. Computer Science (CS) Bachelor+Master in Germany: Difficulty Comparison

If you're a Japanese student deciding between dentistry (Zahnmedizin) and a Computer Science (CS) Bachelor+Master in Germany, here's a detailed comparison of admission difficulty, costs, language requirements, and job prospects.

1. Admission Difficulty

Factor Dental MD (Zahnmedizin) CS Bachelor+Master
Total Duration 6 years (State Exam) 5 years (3y Bachelor + 2y Master)
Competition Extremely high (NC-restricted, limited spots for non-EU). Moderate (Most public unis have no NC, but top schools like TUM/ETH are competitive).
GPA Requirement 1.0-1.5 (German scale) ≈ top 5-10% of applicants. 2.5-3.0 (German scale) acceptable at many universities.
Entrance Exam TMS (Test for Medical Studies) often required. None (some unis require GRE or subject tests).
Non-EU Quota Only 5-8% of spots reserved for non-EU students. No quota (all applicants compete equally).
Alternative Pathways Studienkolleg (1 year) if high school diploma isn't recognized. Direct admission possible with a relevant high school diploma.

✅ CS Bachelor+Master is easier to get into (no NC, no strict quotas, lower GPA requirements).
❌ Dental MD is much harder (limited seats, high GPA/TMS demands).

2. Language Requirements

Factor Dental MD CS Bachelor+Master
Language of Study 100% German (C1 level required). Many English-taught Master's programs (B2/C1 English OK).
Bachelor's usually in German (B2 level).
Certification Needed TestDaF (4x4), DSH-2, or Goethe C1. IELTS (~6.5) or TOEFL (~90) for English programs. Some German programs require B2/C1 German.

✅ CS is easier for non-German speakers (many English Master's options).
❌ Dentistry requires near-native German fluency.

3. Tuition & Costs

Factor Dental MD CS Bachelor+Master
Public Universities €0 tuition (only semester fees: €150-€400/semester). €0 tuition (same as dentistry).
Baden-Württemberg €1,500/semester for non-EU (Heidelberg, Freiburg, etc.). €1,500/semester for non-EU (same rule).
Private Universities €5,000-€15,000/semester (e.g., Dresden IMU). €10,000-€20,000/year (rare, e.g., Jacobs University).
Living Costs €850-€1,200/month (similar for both). €850-€1,200/month (same).

✅ Similar costs, but CS has more free English options at Master's level.

4. Job Prospects After Graduation

Factor Dental MD CS Bachelor+Master
Demand in Germany High (shortage of dentists, especially in rural areas). Very High (tech industry booming, skilled workers needed).
Salary Range €60,000-€80,000 (starting), up to €150,000+ with specialization. €50,000-€70,000 (starting), up to €100,000+ in top tech roles.
Language Dependency Must speak C1 German (patient interaction). Many English-speaking tech jobs (German helps but not always required).
Visa/Work Permit Easy (dentists qualify for EU Blue Card). Very Easy (IT jobs are prioritized for visas).

✅ Both fields have strong job markets, but CS offers more English-friendly opportunities.

Which Is Right for You?

Choose Dentistry (Zahnmedizin) If:

  • You love medicine/hands-on patient care.
  • You're willing to spend 6+ years studying in German.
  • You aim for long-term stability in healthcare.

Choose Computer Science If:

  • You prefer tech/logic over biology/chemistry.
  • You want English-taught options at Master's level.
  • You prefer more flexible career paths.
